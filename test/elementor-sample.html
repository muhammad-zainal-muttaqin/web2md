How We Rebuilt Our Coffee Roaster

When the old drum roaster finally gave out last winter, we decided not to replace it but to rebuild the entire machine from the burner up, documenting every step for anyone who wants to try the same thing at home.

The first job was stripping the chassis down to bare steel, because years of chaff and oil had baked onto every surface and no amount of scrubbing was going to make the old paint safe to reuse near food.

We then re-machined the drum bearings, swapped the analog thermocouple for a pair of digital probes, and wired everything back through a small controller so the roast profile could be logged automatically on every batch.

After three weekends of work the roaster came back to life, and the very first batch of Ethiopian naturals came out cleaner and more even than anything the original machine had ever produced for us.
