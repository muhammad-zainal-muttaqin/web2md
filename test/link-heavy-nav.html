The Quiet Power of Boring Technology

Every few years a new framework promises to make the old way of building software obsolete, and every few years most teams quietly discover that the boring, well-understood tools were the ones that kept shipping working products.

Boring technology is not technology that is bad or outdated; it is technology whose failure modes are so well documented that an engineer can reason about them at three in the morning without guessing.

The cost of novelty is rarely the learning curve itself. It is the long tail of unknown edge cases, the missing answers on forums, and the libraries that were abandoned the moment their original author changed jobs.
